At the highest level, we separate spending into two categories: Discretionary and Fixed.

Discretionary spending is the key measure of your plan. All changes to your plan either raise or lower your discretionary spending. MaxiFi finds your highest path of discretionary spending while maintaining your household's living standard through time. If the discretionary spending level isn’t the same from year to year, it’s because either 1) household expenses went down due to death or kids leaving home or 2) you face what is called a “cash constraint.” Learn More About Cash Constraints

Fixed spending includes all the spending you’ve told us about, including Housing expenses and retirement account contributions. In addition, we’ve calculated annual household taxes and Medicare Part B premium. The Other Expenses category includes 529 account contributions and spending, health savings account employee contributions, reserve fund contributions, QCDs, funeral expenses, bequests, and any Special Expenses you entered.
